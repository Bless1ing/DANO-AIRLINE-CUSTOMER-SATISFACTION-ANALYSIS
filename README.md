# DANO-AIRLINE-CUSTOMER-SATISFACTION-ANALYSIS

![](https://github.com/Bless1ing/DANO-AIRLINE-CUSTOMER-SATISFACTION-ANALYSIS/blob/master/WhatsApp%20Image%203.jpeg)

## REPORT

After analyzing 129,880 passenger records, I found that Dano Airlines has a satisfaction rate of only 43.45%, confirming management's concern that satisfaction has fallen below 50%.

## IMPORTANT FINDINGS

The problem is not one single issue, Customer dissatisfaction is being driven by a combination of

1. Poor online boarding experience
2. Weak in-flight entertainment experience
3. Seat comfort concerns
4. Poor economy-class experience
5. Lower satisfaction among first-time customers
6. Flight delays


The data was cleaned and the “Arrival Delay” column contained missing values.

I replaced missing values appropriately while minimizing distortion and no significant duplicate passenger records affecting analysis were identified.

## Customer Satisfaction by Travel Class
#### Critical Insight

Economy passengers are overwhelmingly dissatisfied,  with them having the lowest satisfaction rating.

Business-class passengers are nearly 4 times more satisfied than Economy passengers and this is the strongest operational issue uncovered.

## Customer Satisfaction by Travel Purpose
#### Findings

Personal travelers are highly dissatisfied, with only about 1 in 10 leisure passengers reporting satisfaction.

This suggests Dano Airlines is currently serving business travelers much better than leisure travelers.

## Customer Satisfaction by Customer Type
#### Findings

First-time customers are far less satisfied.
This is dangerous because dissatisfied first-time passengers are unlikely to become loyal customers.


## Impact of Delays
#### Findings

Dissatisfied passengers experience longer delays.
Although delays are not the only issue, they contribute significantly to customer dissatisfaction.

### What Separates Satisfied and Dissatisfied Customers?

I compared service ratings between satisfied and dissatisfied passengers, with the Strongest Drivers of Satisfaction as
1.	Online Boarding
2.	In-flight Entertainment
3.	Seat Comfort
4.	On-board Service
5.	Leg Room Service
6.	Cleanliness and 
7.	In-flight Wi-Fi

## Interpretation

The largest difference is in Online Boarding. 

Satisfied passengers rated Online Boarding much higher than dissatisfied passengers.

This makes Online Boarding the strongest predictor of satisfaction in the dataset.


## Recommendations
### 1. Improve the Online Boarding Experience
   
This is the single largest driver of satisfaction which will cause very high impact

#### Actions:
- Redesign mobile check-in process
- Reduce boarding wait times
- Improve boarding communication
- Enhance app usability

### 2. Upgrade Economy-Class Experience
   
Economy satisfaction is critically low.

#### Actions:
- Improve seat comfort
- Increase legroom where possible
- Improve food offerings
- Enhance customer service

### 3. Invest in Better In-flight Entertainment and Wi-Fi
    
Both services show strong relationships with customer satisfaction.

#### Actions:
- Upgrade Wi-Fi reliability
- Improve entertainment content library
- Enhance streaming quality

### 4. Reduce Flight Delays

#### Actions:
- Improve scheduling efficiency
- Enhance turnaround operations
- Strengthen delay communication

### 5. Focus on First-Time Customers

#### Actions:
- Welcome programs
- Better onboarding communication
- First-flight support initiatives

## Conclusion

Dano Airlines' satisfaction crisis is primarily driven by poor experiences among:
- Economy passengers
- Personal travelers
- First-time customers

The strongest operational factors affecting satisfaction are:
1. Online Boarding
2. In-flight Entertainment
3. Seat Comfort
4. Leg Room Service
5. Wi-Fi Quality

If management prioritizes these five areas, particularly the economy-class experience and online boarding process, customer satisfaction should improve significantly and move back above the 50% threshold.
